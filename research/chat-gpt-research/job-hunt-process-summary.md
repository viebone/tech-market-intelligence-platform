# Senior UX Individual Contributor Job Search Journey

## 1. Trigger & Reflection

**Purpose:** Understand why a job search is necessary and define what "success" means.

**Typical Triggers**

* Redundancy or layoffs
* Lack of growth or promotion opportunities
* Burnout or dissatisfaction
* Desire for better compensation or impact

**Key Questions**

* Why am I looking?
* What kind of role do I want?
* What matters most to me?

**Outputs**

* Personal goals
* Career criteria
* Definition of success

---

## 2. Strategy & Mindset

**Purpose:** Establish a structured and resilient approach to the search.

**Focus Areas**

* Growth mindset
* Realistic expectations
* Funnel thinking
* Networking strategy
* Metrics and goals

**Frameworks**

* SMART goals
* Job-search funnel
* Design Thinking
* 80/20 networking principle

**Outputs**

* Search strategy
* Weekly targets
* Priority regions and companies

---

## 3. Readiness

**Purpose:** Prepare all career assets before applying.

**Assets**

* CV/Resume
* Portfolio and case studies
* LinkedIn profile
* Cover letter templates

**Best Practices**

* ATS optimization
* Quantified impact
* Strong storytelling
* Tailored positioning

**Outputs**

* Master resume
* Portfolio website
* LinkedIn optimization
* Role-specific variants

---

## 4. Job Discovery

**Purpose:** Identify and prioritize high-quality opportunities.

**Sources**

* LinkedIn
* UX Jobs Board
* Dribbble
* AIGA
* Remote job platforms
* Referrals and networking

**Focus**

* Quality over quantity
* Target companies
* Hidden market opportunities

**Outputs**

* Job pipeline
* Company watchlists
* Networking contacts

---

## 5. Application

**Purpose:** Convert opportunities into interview invitations.

**Activities**

* Tailor CV and portfolio
* Write cover letters
* Apply systematically
* Track progress

**Metrics**

* Applications sent
* Response rate
* Interview conversion

**Tools**

* Job trackers
* Notion
* Teal
* ATS keyword tools

**Outputs**

* Customized applications
* Application tracker
* Interview pipeline

---

## 6. Interview

**Purpose:** Demonstrate capability, impact and fit.

**Typical Stages**

1. Recruiter screen
2. Hiring manager interview
3. Portfolio presentation
4. Panel interviews
5. Final round

**Preparation**

* STAR stories
* Portfolio walkthrough
* Case studies
* Mock interviews

**Outputs**

* Interview notes
* Design exercises
* Follow-up communications

---

## 7. Offer & Negotiation

**Purpose:** Evaluate opportunities and maximize long-term value.

**Evaluation Criteria**

* Compensation
* Equity and benefits
* Team and culture
* Career growth
* Scope and influence

**Activities**

* Salary negotiation
* Offer comparison
* Risk assessment

**Outputs**

* Accepted offer
* Negotiated package
* Transition plan

---

## 8. Decision & Transition

**Purpose:** Successfully move into the new role.

**Activities**

* Resignation
* Knowledge transfer
* Onboarding preparation
* First 90-day planning

**Outputs**

* Exit plan
* Learning plan
* Early success strategy

---

# Underlying Principle

The UX job search should be managed like a product:

**Inputs → Process → Metrics → Iteration → Outcomes**

Success depends not only on the quality of the candidate, but also on maintaining a healthy funnel, continuously improving materials, leveraging networking, and making data-driven decisions throughout the journey.
